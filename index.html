Deployment Reality

Most deployment failures don’t begin in hardware. They begin in assumptions — about control logic, utilisation, integration responsibility, and field readiness. What performs in pilot conditions often breaks under fleet scale.

What gets protected
  • Decisions that can be defended.
  • Fleet utilisation and operability.
  • Support burden, spares, and field reliability.
Where systems lose alignment Controls, commercial framing, and site behaviour drift apart. The result is under‑utilised assets, support blowouts, and slow loss of confidence inside the fleet.

Utilisation mismatch

Load profiles, duty cycles, and operator habits aren’t reflected in the design intent.

Integration ambiguity

No single owner for controls behaviour, change control, or boundary responsibilities.

Support readiness gap

Spares, diagnostics, training, and escalation paths arrive after the first failure.
